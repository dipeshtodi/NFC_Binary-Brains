Frequently Asked Questions

Question 1:How can I legally minimize my taxes?

To legally minimize your taxes in India, you can explore various deductions, exemptions, and credits available under the Income Tax Act. This includes investments in tax-saving instruments like Provident Funds, Equity-Linked Saving Schemes (ELSS), National Pension System (NPS), and more.

Question 2: What are the most common tax deductions and credits available to individuals?

Common deductions include those under Section 80C (like PF, PPF, ELSS), Section 80D (for health insurance premiums), and Section 24 (for home loan interest). Tax credits can be availed through TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) and Advance Tax payments.

Question 3: What is the difference between tax deductions and tax credits?

Tax deductions reduce your taxable income, whereas tax credits directly reduce your tax liability. For example, if you have a deduction of ₹1,00,000, your taxable income is reduced by that amount. On the other hand, a tax credit of ₹10,000 directly reduces your final tax payable.

Question 4: How can I maximize my deductions and credits?

To maximize deductions, consider investing in eligible instruments like PPF, ELSS, NSC, and taking advantage of home loan interest. Additionally, ensure you claim all eligible exemptions and allowances. To optimize credits, stay updated with your TDS and pay Advance Tax if applicable.

Question 5: Are there any specific tax-saving strategies for self-employed individuals or freelancers?

Self-employed individuals can benefit from deductions under Section 80CCD(1B) for additional NPS contributions. Freelancers can also claim expenses related to their profession as deductions, such as office rent, utilities, and professional fees.

Question 6: What records and documents do I need to keep for tax purposes?

It's essential to maintain records of income, investments, expenses, and supporting documents like bank statements, salary slips, rent receipts, and investment proofs. This helps in accurate tax filing and reduces the chances of errors during an audit.
